Madura Microfinance

FOUNDED IN 2005
 Elevar’s Entry in 2008
Board Member: Sandeep Farias
maduramicrofinance.com

Madura is a highly efficient, customer focused microfinance institution that serves economically active, rural women in Tamil Nadu


Company Overview

Founded by the late Dr. Thiagarajan, a respected banker and businessman from Chennai, Madura is now run by his daughter, Dr. Tara Thiagarajan (Chairman) and an experienced management team. Madura has been one of the most cost efficient microfinance institutions worldwide. The company’s objective is to provide cost efficient microfinance coupled with knowledge and information services that raise the human and organizational capability of its borrowers, improving the effectiveness of loans so that borrowers make the most productive use of credit.

Rural Tamil Nadu represents one the largest markets in India with a population of 40 million, 40% of whom live below the poverty line.

Community Served

Rural Tamil Nadu represents one the largest markets in India with a population of 40 million, 40% of whom live below the poverty line. Tamil Nadu’s GDP growth rate has been over 10% in recent years, but rural GDP growth in the state is less than half this rate at 4-5% per annum. Madura serves self-assembled groups of 15-20 rural women who come together in local communities, guarantee each other’s loans and form a learning and support group for one another.

However, Shubham has focused on opening branches in urban and peri-urban low income communities with high stability (stable base of residents for multiple years) and high degrees of entrepreneurialism. An improved home has direct and positive impact on the health, security and self-esteem of homeowners and their families.

Entrepreneurs

Tara Thiagarajan, Chairperson, is primarily a scientist whose interests encompass understanding complex systems and using a science based approach to solve large scale human problems through scalable for-profit enterprise. She has brought her own ideas and knowledge of economic development in rural India to drive customer-centric strategy and holistic product approach. She has an MBA from the Kellogg School of Management where she was an Austin scholar.

M. Narayanan, CEO, has 25 years of experience in the banking industry where he has held key positions as Head of Treasury at Bank of Madura and Head of Treasury at the GMR Group, Bangalore. His earlier career led him through a variety of significant roles at Bank of Madura including Head of Mumbai – Main, the largest branch of the bank, and AGM of Cash Management Services, Merchant Banking and Corporate Banking.

Elevar's Take

Madura is amongst the most cost efficient MFIs in the World. Their additional services in form of knowledge and access help increase income levels of their clients

Given our knowledge of microcredit models, Elevar understood the value of Madura’s low cost operating structure and focus on deepening relationships with customers by providing more products and services to unleash human capital and human capacity. Madura was one among the few MFIs that were compliant with regulatory changes for the microfinance sector (e.g. interest rate caps, margin caps) that were recommended post the Andhra Pradesh microfinance crisis of 2010, even before they were implemented.

Elevar was the first institutional investor in Madura and has been a key stakeholder through several phases of growth.

Elevar was a founding investor at Vistaar - and also committed capital through a phase of crisis in the industry (triggered by the microfinance crisis in India) to help the company pivot its business model successfully.
