Varthana

FOUNDED IN 2013
 Elevar’s Entry in 2013
Board Member: Jyotsna Krishnan
varthana.com

Varthana provides infrastructure and project loans to help affordable private school entrepreneurs grow and improve their schools.


Company Overview

Varthana empowers schools that have local credibility and a strong track record within their communities by offering secured and unsecured loans (based on a cash-flow underwriting model that is customized for this segment). These loans are used for a variety of projects: new classroom space, dedicated bathrooms for girls, computer and science labs, benches, desks and new didactic materials to name a few.

About 40% of all enrolled students in India study in affordable private schools, some with fees as low as $5 a month.

Community Served

Affordable private school entrepreneurs in India with credibility in their communities – schools that charge a monthly fee of US$5 – US$25 per student. There are over 200,000 such schools working with over 80 million students.

Entrepreneurs

Steve Hardgrave, CEO, brings a unique mix of operating experience, entrepreneurship, international development and investment expertise. He played a key role in conceptualization, launch and oversight of Indian School Finance Company (ISFC). He successfully built early stage social venture portfolios for Gray Ghost Ventures and Omidyar Network. Prior to that, he was founder and CEO of a successful Mexican microfinance institution.

Brajesh Mishra, COO, was a founding team member of ISFC, responsible for developing core operating processes. Later, he took over as CEO and started driving diversification of the product range, optimization of credit parameters, broadening of the marketing and raising debt capital for the company. Before this, he spent 14 years with ICICI Bank in the retail finance sector, car and two wheeler loans and rural and agri-banking space.

Elevar's Take

A thoughtfully constructed, scalable commercial business model to address a niche underserved market – one that has the potential to create cascading impact across thousands of schools, hundreds of thousands of teachers and millions of low income school children

Varthana is Elevar’s first investment from Elevar Equity III – and follows the core investment thesis of identifying large underserved communities that can be addressed through commercial business models. Varthana is unique in that it targets the education sector through a financial services model, thereby overcoming several inherent challenges faced by education ventures with respect to achieving scale.

The team has displayed resilience and strong determination since inception: setting up the business and building out the business model and organization, leveraging initial equity by raising commercial debt prior to achieving profitability. The financial services business has the additional potential of leveraging its distribution channel to offer non-financial services to its network of school customers. This would further strengthen Varthana’s relationships with school entrepreneurs and pave the way to enhancing the quality of education in a profitable manner.
