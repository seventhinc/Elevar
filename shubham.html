Shubham Housing Finance

FOUNDED IN 2010
 Elevar’s Entry in 2010
Board Member: Sandeep Farias
shubham.co

Shubham provides home improvement and new home loans to low income individuals and families in urban and semi-urban India.


Company Overview

Shubham focuses on customers who face challenges accessing finance from mainstream lenders due to the informal nature of their income. Distinct among the few housing finance companies serving lower income groups, Shubham will focus a portion of its operations on the home-improvement loan category in addition to new home loans. The company currently operates in multiple states in India.

The population for low income home improvement loans is estimated at 34.2 million households.

Community Served

Large amounts of economic activity take place in slum populations within urban India. Many residents not only have some credit history, but also own their homes. However, these individuals continue to lack access to any type of credit. Typically, low income clients have been excluded from the mortgage market because of a lack of formal income, savings history, credit history and documentation.

However, Shubham has focused on opening branches in urban and peri-urban low income communities with high stability (stable base of residents for multiple years) and high degrees of entrepreneurialism. An improved home has direct and positive impact on the health, security and self-esteem of homeowners and their families.

Elevar's Take

Unique approach to housing finance products for low income communities and credit appraisal process - an approach that allows customers to buy homes and live in their existing communities as against relocate to special ‘affordable housing projects’ on the outskirts of towns

Shubham presented Elevar with an opportunity to invest in the very large and highly underserved market for housing finance to low income communities. It also stands out with its unique credit underwriting approach customized to suit the informal segment being targeted. Our research shows that between 50-70% of microfinance borrowers already own their own homes in their communities, indicating a very large market opportunity in this segment. Shubham’s focus on urban and peri-urban India also allows it to address financing for an estimated urban housing shortfall of 15 million units for low income households.

Elevar invested in the company at the formation stage and with a little over one and a half years of receiving its housing finance license, Shubham raised the Series B and Series C rounds with participation from existing and new investors - Saama Capital , Accion and Motilal Owsal Private Equity. It has successfully set up branches across the Northern and Western States of India and become profitable. The company mitigates title risks by leveraging the dynamics and perceptions of property titles in the low income housing segment with a methodology that is unique to Shubham. The management’s ability to raise debt, critical to the success of any finance company, and their achievement of important milestones including early branch profitability demonstrate a robust operating model to meet the housing finance needs of low income communities.
