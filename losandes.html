Caja Rural Los Andes

Founded in 1997
 Initial investment in 2010
Board member: Johanna Posada
cajarurallosandes.com

‘Los Andes’ vision is to create a leading microfinance institution of scale, and the pre-eminent provider of financial services in Peru’s rural market’


Company overview

Los Andes is a profitable, deposit-taking, regulated finance company that caters to the needs of the rural community in the southeast Andean region of Peru. It is positioned as a leading community bank with a recognizable, trustworthy brand, strong customer focus, cost-efficient culture and a unique credit model focused on high quality, underserved rural customers.

Community served

Los Andes’ focus is on micro-entrepreneurs/enterprises operating in the dynamic rural Peruvian markets with a deep culture of entrepreneurship & cohesive social and economic ties. This includes male and female producers involved in livestock, trout farming, cheese making and other non-farm agricultural activities. Generally, Los Andes’ customers are involved in several economic activities so as to be able to diversify income.

The Peruvian microfinance credit market is estimated at $6 billion.

Entrepreneur

Rosanna Ramos Velita, Chairman of the Board, came back to her Peruvian roots after a successful professional journey in international finance. She has over 20 years of experience in international banking (senior management at Citigroup’s global consumer banking) and in Latin America investment banking (UBS). Rosanna is a Member of the Board of Grameen Foundation where she is currently Chairman of the Latin American Council and also serves as a Member of the Wharton Executive Board for Latin America and is a Member of the Board of Enseña Perú, the Peruvian chapter of Teach for America.
