aarusha.com

Aarusha provides safe, well-priced, high value rental housing solutions to low income migrants in urban India.


Company Overview

Aarusha manages hostels that offer shared accommodation and better value to its male and female residents (primarily students, first-time workers, and entry level job seekers migrating from rural India to urban centers). Value proposition of Aarusha hostels is security, cleanliness, good food, high quality beds and furniture at competitive pricing.

It is estimated that migrants in India constitute around 32% of the urban population

Community Served

There are approximately 113 million migrants in Indian cities who move to urban areas due to lack of rural education and employment opportunities, declining investment in agriculture and a desire for better prospects. The majority of this segment has little or no access to organized rental housing and is serviced by unorganized local players who provide minimal service and poor quality accommodations. Aarusha is addressing the need of these young students and workers migrating from rural to urban India for better education and job opportunities. Nevertheless, housing is an essential need for migrants and one that they spend time and energy trying to find at best value as it consumes a large share of wallet.

Entrepreneurs

V. Satyanarayana (Satya) is an urban development expert with tremendous experience creating formal markets, structures and systems where none have existed. Previously, he worked as a finance advisor to the United Nations and to USAID working on projects in slum upgrading and urban infrastructure development. He was involved in the implementation of state and national reforms for improved housing and infrastructure in Asia and Africa.

Dyan Belliappa, Co-Founder, is a trained, hands-on architect and an urban planner with more than 20 years of experience in designing and managing housing projects. Dyan holds a master’s degree in urban and regional planning from CEPT, Ahmedabad and a bachelor’s degree in architecture from REC, Nagpur.

Elevar's Take

Unique, migrant-centric company that provides an essential service at better value than existing options; potential to create a national brand around providing housing solutions to migrant students and workers in India

The migration of rural individuals to urban centers is well documented by academic research. One of the greatest challenges faced by migrants is finding safe, affordable, and comfortable rental housing in a new city as existing providers are highly fragmented, unorganized and often exploitative. Based on his work creating urban infrastructure and more recently, upgrading slum communities, Satya observed the opportunity to provide a valuable service for which migrants are willing to pay a substantial portion of their monthly earnings. Driven by the desire to offer better beds, safer environments, and higher quality food at market average price, he sought to bring better and smarter management to the low-income hostel market in India.

Satya’s efforts coincided with work that Elevar had undertaken to develop an investment theme around migrant services, where we recognized that migrants face high transaction costs and poor quality when it came to housing options in addition to challenges accessing financial services. We understand the opportunity to build a strong hostel management company focused on a very large market – migrant students and workers – and a secular trend of urbanization that will continue for years to come. Aarusha’s dedicated management and strong focus on the drivers of profitability in its business, given low price points per bed, are key ingredients to achieve scale and high quality service levels.
